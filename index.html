Patriots of Bengal

স্বাধীনতার সৈনিক

Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose

নেতাজি সুভাষচন্দ্র বসু

1897 – 1945

A fierce nationalist whose defiant patriotism made him a hero in India. He raised the Azad Hind Fauj and gave the clarion call, "Give me blood and I will give you freedom!"

Khudiram Bose

ক্ষুদিরাম বসু

1889 – 1908

One of the youngest revolutionaries of the Indian independence movement. He walked to the gallows with a smile, cementing his place in the folklore of Bengal.

বাঘা যতীন

1879 – 1915
Jatindranath Mukherjee earned his moniker by allegedly killing a tiger bare-handed. A visionary leader of the Jugantar party who died fighting the British forces.

Bipin Chandra Pal

বিপিন চন্দ্র পাল

1858 – 1932

One third of the "Lal Bal Pal" triumvirate. A brilliant orator and writer who was one of the main architects of the Swadeshi movement.

Aurobindo Ghose

অরবিন্দ ঘোষ

1872 – 1950

From a revolutionary leader in Bengal to a spiritual reformer in Pondicherry. His writings ignited the flame of militant nationalism among the youth.

Pritilata Waddedar

প্রীতিলতা ওয়াদ্দেদার

1911 – 1932

A fiery revolutionary who led the armed attack on the Pahartali European Club. She chose martyrdom over surrender, inspiring generations of women.

Food

খাবার

Rosogolla

Pillowy spheres of fresh chhena (cottage cheese) simmered in a delicate, light syrup until they achieve a signature airy, melt-in-the-mouth texture.

Mishti Doi

CArtisanal yogurt slow-fermented in traditional clay pots, resulting in a thick, caramelized sweetness with a distinct velvety finish.

Kathi Roll

Flaky parathas wrapped around spiced kebabs.

Hilsa Fish Curry

The 'Queen of Fish' prepared in its most authentic form: raw steaks simmered in a spicy, sinus-clearing mustard paste and finished with a sharp drizzle of raw mustard oil

The original Kolkata street wrap: smoky, charcoal-grilled kebabs encased in a crisp, layered paratha with a hit of lime and green chilies.
Puchka

Crisp, hollow semolina spheres filled with a spiced mashed potato mix and dunked in tangy, 'gondhoraj' lime-infused tamarind water for an explosive burst of flavor.

Festivals

উৎসব

Druga Puja

The heartbeat of Bengal: a week-long carnival of monumental art installations (Pandals), rhythmic drumbeats (Dhaki), and the homecoming of the Goddess.

Kali Puja

A powerful midnight celebration of the 'Dark Mother'—illuminated by thousands of clay lamps (diyas) and vibrant firework displays.

Saraswati Puja

A sea of yellow: students pay homage to the Goddess of Knowledge, marks the onset of Spring, and is famously known as the Bengali Valentine’s Day."

Pahela Baishakh

The Bengali New Year: a fresh start marked by traditional attire, 'Haalkhata' (new account books) for businesses, and grand family feasts.

Christmas

A glittering legacy of the British era: Park Street transforms into a neon-lit wonderland filled with live music, plum cakes, and carnival spirit.

Bhai Phonta/h5>

A sacred ceremony of sibling bonds where sisters apply a 'phonta' (tilak) on their brothers' foreheads, praying for their longevity and protection.

he soul-stirring songs of Bengal’s wandering mystics, performed with the 'Ektara' to spread a message of universal love and spiritual freedom.

Rabindra Sangeet

The vast musical legacy of Rabindranath Tagore; a genre that maps the entire spectrum of human emotion through poetic lyrics and timeless melodies.

Nazrul Geeti

The revolutionary and high-energy compositions of Kazi Nazrul Islam, ranging from fiery freedom songs to soulful devotional ghazals.
